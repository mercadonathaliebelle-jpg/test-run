Who WE Are

From Us, For All is a student-led advocacy initiative inspired by SDG 4: Quality Education. Our group was born out of the belief that education should be accessible and equitable for everyone—no matter the background or circumstance. What started as small acts of peer tutoring and community teaching soon grew into a platform that amplifies awareness and mobilizes youth-led action for education reform.

Our story reflects the power of collaboration among young leaders and advocates. We believe that true progress happens when students themselves take part in shaping the solutions to education challenges. Through this website, we hope to inspire others—students, educators, parents, and citizens—to take simple yet impactful steps toward building a future where education is inclusive, fair, and empowering.

Meet the Team

Nathalie Belle Mercado

Founder of From Us, For All

I am Nathalie Belle Mercado, a student leader from Philippine Science High School Southern Mindanao Campus and a volunteer teacher in Likharal, a community-based initiative where students dedicate their time to mentoring and supporting younger learners. Through this experience, I have seen firsthand how education can empower lives, but also how deeply unequal access remains across communities.

Motivated by these realities and inspired by the strength of collaboration, I co-founded From Us, For All alongside equally passionate peers. Our initiative was born from the belief that young people are not just future leaders—we are changemakers today. By spotlighting the education crisis in the Philippines, we aim to amplify awareness, inspire action, and emphasize that youth voices matter in shaping solutions. While the project presents our story in a fictionalized way, it reflects the real efforts of countless student advocates nationwide who are bridging learning gaps, standing in solidarity with underserved communities, and proving that every small action can spark lasting change.

Our Story

The story of From Us, For All began in quiet conversations after classes, where a handful of students shared a common frustration: the gap between what education could be and what it often was for many Filipino learners. Nathalie Belle Mercado, driven by her leadership experience in Philippine Science High School Southern Mindanao Campus and her volunteer work in Likharal, carried with her countless stories of children who longed to learn but lacked the tools to do so. Her passion attracted others—friends who had witnessed the same struggles in their own communities, each carrying their own reason for joining the cause. Together, they realized that if they pooled their skills, creativity, and voices, they could create something larger than themselves.

The team was built on diversity of strengths, each member bringing something vital to the initiative:

  • The Researchers – who dig deep into data about the education crisis, turning numbers into knowledge that guides the movement.
  • The Creatives – who design visuals, write content, and shape campaigns that capture hearts and inspire others to act.
  • The Organizers – who coordinate projects, events, and outreach to ensure that every plan turns into meaningful action.
  • The Connectors – who build relationships with schools, youth groups, and local communities, keeping the initiative rooted in real needs.

As the team grew, so did their vision. Every role mattered, and every contribution became a thread in a larger fabric of advocacy. The group adopted the name From Us, For All to remind themselves and others that change begins with young people, and that their efforts—no matter how small—were meant to ripple outward to reach every learner, especially those most often overlooked.

What made the team unique was not just their commitment, but their bond. They worked across differences—balancing heavy academics, personal struggles, and the weight of being young advocates in a world that often underestimated them. Yet in late-night brainstorming sessions, community visits, and schoolyard gatherings, they discovered strength in unity. Their fictionalized story, shared through From Us, For All, represents not only their journey but also the countless unnamed student leaders across the country who continue to rise for education. The team’s message remains clear: when young people act together, they can transform the narrative of education from one of inequality to one of possibility.
