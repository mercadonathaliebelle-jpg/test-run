Background of the Problem

Quality education is a fundamental right, yet it remains out of reach for many. Globally, UNESCO reports that 250 million children and youth are out of school, and millions more attend classes but do not achieve basic literacy and numeracy skills. In the Philippines, UNICEF highlights that 90% of ten-year-olds cannot read simple text, and 83% lack minimum proficiency in math. The Functional Literacy, Education and Mass Media Survey (FLEMMS) also shows that while basic literacy is at 93.1%, functional literacy is only 70.8%, which reveals gaps in comprehension and application of knowledge. These statistics underscore a national learning crisis that hinders the development of students and the country’s future workforce.

Local issues worsen the problem: regional inequalities leave areas like Bangsamoro with far lower literacy rates, while lack of digital access during the pandemic excluded many students from continued learning. Recently, environmental challenges such as extreme heat waves caused repeated class suspensions, disrupting learning for millions of students. These barriers collectively make it difficult for the Philippines to achieve SDG 4’s target of inclusive and equitable quality education.

What is Currently Being Done?

The Philippines has made strides in addressing the education crisis through the Department of Education’s Alternative Learning System (ALS), which offers flexible pathways for out-of-school children, youth, and adults to complete basic education. Since its implementation, millions of learners have accessed ALS, gaining opportunities to pursue higher education or employment. UNICEF has also partnered with DepEd to enhance ALS with digital tools, psychosocial learning, and innovations like ICT4ALS. These efforts reflect a strong push for inclusion and adaptability in the country’s educational framework.

Beyond government and NGO programs, grassroots initiatives are playing a role. One example is Likharal, a community-based teaching initiative where students volunteer their time to teach and mentor younger learners. Programs like Likharal prove that education advocacy is not limited to policymakers and institutions—ordinary students can also contribute by sharing their knowledge, time, and creativity. Together, these formal and grassroots solutions show that progress toward SDG 4 is possible when both systems and communities act hand in hand.

Success Stories & Highlights

Likharal

Empowering Future Educators

Likharal is a student-led initiative at PSHS-SMC that tutors Grade 5 learners in Math, Science, English, and Research. It boosts academic skills, confidence, and curiosity, giving students guidance while fostering creativity and a love for learning.

Alternative Learning System (ALS)

Flexible Education for All

The ALS provides out-of-school youth and adults with flexible pathways to complete basic education. Combining traditional instruction with digital tools and psychosocial support, it ensures learners in underserved areas gain literacy, numeracy, and life skills, empowering them to pursue higher studies or employment.

GalaXsikat

Young Astronomers

GalaXsikat is a student-led program at PSHS-SMC that introduces elementary students to astronomy and space science through workshops, stargazing, and hands-on activities. It sparks curiosity, nurtures scientific thinking, and inspires future careers in STEM fields.

Hayagmaon

Youth Empowerment and Civic Engagement

Hayagmaon, led by PSHS-SMC scholars, conducts outreach for resource-limited elementary students in Davao City. Through tutoring, mentorship, and civic engagement initiatives, it fosters equitable access to education while cultivating socially responsible youth who actively contribute to their communities.

Project DELTA

Digital Literacy for Elementary Students

Project DELTA is a student-led non-profit at PSHS-SMC focused on teaching elementary students computer science. By providing coding lessons, computational thinking exercises, and problem-solving activities, it equips learners with essential digital skills for academic and future career success.

Pamulaan Center for Indigenous Peoples Education

Pamulaan provides culturally relevant education for Indigenous children, youth, and adults. By integrating traditional knowledge with formal learning, it preserves cultural heritage while giving students literacy, numeracy, and life skills for long-term personal and community growth.

Angiskul ma Bangka (AmB)

Zamboanga Outreach

AmB serves internally displaced Indigenous Bajau children in Zamboanga City, offering creative and engaging educational experiences. The program ensures access to quality education while respecting cultural identity, fostering both academic and personal development.

Unang Hakbang

Palawan Indigenous Education

Unang Hakbang equips Indigenous children in Palawan with literacy and numeracy skills. Beyond academics, it builds confidence, independence, and a solid foundation for continued learning, empowering them to thrive in school and beyond.

IPED

Integrated Program for Educational Development

IPED implements community-based programs to improve educational outcomes in underprivileged areas. Through tutoring, mentorship, and resource provision, it ensures children gain access to quality education while developing critical skills for lifelong learning.

AY Foundation

Scholarships and Outreach

The AY Foundation provides scholarships, school supplies, and community-based educational programs for marginalized youth. Its initiatives improve access to quality education and empower students to pursue their academic and personal goals.

GMA Kapuso Foundation

Bridging Education Gaps

The GMA Kapuso Foundation delivers outreach programs, scholarships, and educational resources to learners in remote and underserved areas. By supporting children and communities, it ensures equitable learning opportunities and fosters long-term educational development.
